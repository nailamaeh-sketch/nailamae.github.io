    	<div class="main">
        	<h2> The elements of climate-resilient infrastructure </h2>
        	<p> <strong>1. Planning and Design –</strong> Use climate science to site projects away from high-risk areas, incorporate flexibility and adaptability, and design for redundancy to maintain operations during disruptions. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong>2. Durable and Low –</strong> Low-Carbon Materials: Use sustainable materials like self-healing and CO₂-absorbing concrete, heat-reflective surfaces, and fire-resistant components. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong>3. Nature-Based Solutions –</strong> Employ wetlands, mangroves,    green roofs, and living shorelines to reduce flood risk, heat, and erosion while supporting biodiversity. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong>4. Early Warning Systems –</strong> Integrate AI, satellite monitoring, IoT, and communication networks for real-time hazard alerts and disaster preparedness. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong>5. Governance and Adaptation – </strong> Establish policies and frameworks for continuous monitoring, adaptive management, and transformative adaptation to evolving climate risks. <br>
		</p>
