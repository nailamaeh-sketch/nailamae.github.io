    	<div class="main">
        	<h2> Examples of effective climate-resilient infrastructure </h2>
        	<p> <strong>NETHERLANDS</strong> - Room for the River: Instead of building higher dykes, the project creates space for rivers to flood safely by moving dykes inland, deepening riverbeds, and creating flood bypasses, reducing flood risk naturally. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong> SINGAPORE </strong> - Cooling Program: The city uses rooftop gardens, street trees, green walls, and light-colored roofs to reduce heat and improve ventilation, helping to combat rapid urban warming. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong> BADAJOZ, SPAIN </strong> - Heat and Drought Prep: A school uses green roofs, shaded facades, rainwater harvesting, and automatic ventilation to lower indoor temperatures and conserve water amid rising heat. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong> CALIFORNIA </strong> - Wildfire Resistance: The region enforces building codes requiring wildfire-resistant materials, limits development in fire-prone areas, and maintains defensible space around homes to reduce fire risk. <br>
		</p>
		<p> <strong> DAVENPORT, IOWA – </strong> - The city has designed riverfront parks and infrastructure that can safely flood, allowing the Mississippi River room to expand without damaging critical infrastructure. <br>
		</p>
